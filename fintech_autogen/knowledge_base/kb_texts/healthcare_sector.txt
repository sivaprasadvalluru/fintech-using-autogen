The healthcare sector includes companies that provide medical services, manufacture medical equipment or drugs, provide medical insurance, or otherwise facilitate the provision of healthcare to patients.
Pharmaceutical companies research, develop, produce, and market medications. They can be focused on brand-name drugs, generics, or both.
Biotechnology companies use cellular and biomolecular processes to develop products that help improve lives. They often focus on novel drug development and clinical research.
Medical device companies design and manufacture medical equipment for patient diagnosis and treatment.
Healthcare providers include hospitals, clinics, and other organizations that provide healthcare services to patients.
Telemedicine uses telecommunications technology to provide healthcare remotely, improving access for patients in rural or underserved areas.
Health informatics is the acquisition, storage, retrieval, and use of healthcare information to foster better collaboration among a patient's various healthcare providers.
Genomics is the study of the complete set of DNA (including all of its genes) in a person or other organism, with applications in personalized medicine.
Wearable health technology includes devices like fitness trackers and smartwatches that monitor health metrics and encourage healthy behaviors.
Healthcare analytics uses data and statistical methods to improve patient outcomes, optimize costs, and streamline operations in healthcare organizations.
Regenerative medicine focuses on developing methods to regrow, repair, or replace damaged or diseased cells, organs, or tissues.
Medical imaging technologies such as MRI, CT scans, and ultrasound are essential for diagnosis and treatment planning.
Healthcare policy shapes the organization, financing, and delivery of healthcare services, impacting access, quality, and cost.
